What is Spouse Visa?

Also known as a UK marriage visa, a spouse visa allows married partners of UK citizens to immigrate to the UK because they are married to someone who is 'settled in the UK' - i.e. a person who is ordinarily resident in the UK and has no immigration restrictions on how long they can stay in the UK.

Spouse Visa only last for 33 months, you will need then to re-apply for a Further Leave to Remain Visa(FLR-M) More Info within the the UK, This will extend your stay in the UK for an additional 30 months by which your stay will total up to 5 years. At this point you can apply for a Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) More Info

Can I work on a spouse visa?

Yes, you are eligible to work or study in the UK once the visa has been granted. You are also eligible to use Public healthcare as this is paid through your IHA and once you are working you'll be paying for National Insurance that pays towards Health care.

If you have British children they are entitled to Child Benefits, this is not considered part of your visa restrictions.

What do you need to qualify?

To qualify for a UK spouse / marriage visa you must satisfy the following requirements:

  • You and your spouse must be 18 years old or over.
  • You must have met each other and be legally married - this is to prevent arranged marriages.
  • You must intend to live together permanently.
  • You must have enough money to support yourselves (and any dependents) without claiming public funds.
  • Your sponsoring partner must earn more than £18,600 per year or have enough savings to be able to sponsor you. The minimum financial requirement is higher if they are also sponsoring dependant children. More Info
    !!! Financial Requirement is subject to change starting Spring 2024 from £18,600 to £29,000 ,with further rise to £38,700 by early 2025.
  • You must have suitable accommodation for you, your spouse and any dependants. More Info
  • You must satisfy the English language requirements. More Info

How do I apply for a spouse visa?

Your partner/husband (sponsor) can submit your application from and documents from within the UK. Although, even if your paper work was submitted within the UK, you will still need to attend the nearest VFS centers for your Biometrics submission

UK Accommodation requirement

The UK property that the applicant and UK partner intend to live in must be ‘owned/rented or occupied exclusively’ by their family. It must also not be ‘overcrowded’, as defined by the Housing Act 1985 More Info. Furthermore, it is essential to note that you will be expected to provide documentation to show that the applicant and sponsor will legally occupy the UK property.

What you need:

  • House Title
  • Mortgage Letter
  • Rental Agreement

  • Council Tax
