What is Spouse Visa?

Also known as a UK marriage visa, a spouse visa allows married partners of UK citizens to immigrate to the UK because they are married to someone who is 'settled in the UK' - i.e. a person who is ordinarily resident in the UK and has no immigration restrictions on how long they can stay in the UK.

Spouse Visa only last for 33 months, you will need then to re-apply for a Further Leave to Remain Visa(FLR-M) More Info within the the UK, This will extend your stay in the UK for an additional 30 months by which your stay will total up to 5 years. At this point you can apply for a Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) More Info

Can I work on a spouse visa?

Yes, you are eligible to work or study in the UK once the visa has been granted. You are also eligible to use Public healthcare as this is paid through your IHA and once you are working you'll be paying for National Insurance that pays towards Health care.

If you have British children they are entitled to Child Benefits, this is not considered part of your visa restrictions.

What do you need to qualify?

To qualify for a UK spouse / marriage visa you must satisfy the following requirements:

  • You and your spouse must be 18 years old or over.
  • You must have met each other and be legally married - this is to prevent arranged marriages.
  • You must intend to live together permanently.
  • You must have enough money to support yourselves (and any dependents) without claiming public funds.
  • Your sponsoring partner must earn more than £18,600 per year or have enough savings to be able to sponsor you. More Info The minimum financial requirement changes when you have a additional dependent.
    • £3,800 a year for your first child
    • £2,400 a year for your first child
      • You may be able to use your savings instead of income. How you prove you have the money depends on how you got the income.
    !!! Financial Requirement is subject to change starting Spring 2024 from £18,600 to £29,000 ,with further rise to £38,700 by early 2025.
  • You must have suitable accommodation for you, your spouse and any dependants. More Info
  • You must satisfy the English language requirements. More Info
  • Tuberculosis Negative Certification. More Info

UK Financial Requirements

The UK partner will need to have an annual income of £18,600 unless:

  1. The UK partner receives any of the following benefits:

    Disability Living Allowance; Severe Disablement Allowance; Industrial Injury Disablement Benefit; Attendance Allowance; Carer’s Allowance; Personal Independence Payment; Armed Forces Independence Payment or Guaranteed Income Payment under the Armed Forces Compensation Scheme; or Constant Attendance Allowance, Mobility Supplement or War Disablement Pension under the War Pensions Scheme.This is because, if the UK partner receives one of the above benefits, the adequate maintenance test will apply instead of the minimum income threshold of £18,600+.

  2. The applicant and/or UK partner has cash savings of £16,000 or more that have been in the applicant’s or UK partner’s control for at least 6 months before the date of application.
    • If the cash savings amounts to £62,500, then this satisfies the financial requirement alone.
    • Anything within the region of £16,000-£62,499 reduces the annual income required.

UK Accommodation requirement

The UK property that the applicant and UK partner intend to live in must be ‘owned/rented or occupied exclusively’ by their family. It must also not be ‘overcrowded’, as defined by the Housing Act 1985 More Info. Furthermore, it is essential to note that you will be expected to provide documentation to show that the applicant and sponsor will legally occupy the UK property.

What you need:

  • House Title
  • Mortgage Letter
  • Rental Agreement
  • Council Tax

UK English language requirement


!!! Note:You do not have to satisfy the English Requirment if you are 65 years old or over.!!!

There are numerous ways to satisfy the English language requirement.

  • Proof or Certification of English-taught academic degree equivalent to a Bachelor’s, Master’s or PhD degree that was awarded outside the UK. IE Diploma
  • IELTS Life Skills A1 test
    • However, if the applicant’s English can be described as ‘intermediate’ or ‘advanced’, it is recommended that they take the ‘A2’ or ‘B1’ test, despite the A1 test being required for a spouse visa. This is because if the applicant relies on an A2 English language test for their first spouse visa application, this will prevent them from having to do the test again for the subsequent FLR M visa in the UK (which requires a test at A2 level or above). If the applicant relies on a B1 English language test for their first or second spouse visa application, they will normally be able to rely on the same test to meet the English language requirement for the Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) application. Applicants will normally be awarded an English language test certificate once they have passed the Secure English language test. This English language test certificate will contain a SELT unique reference number (URN) which will be required as part of your spouse visa application (if you rely on the English language test to satisfy the English language requirement).

Spouse visa supporting letters

Including supporting letters from both the applicant and their partner can strengthen the application and/or avoid potential misunderstandings.

In these supporting letters, you and your partner can write about the following:

  • How the financial requirement is met
  • How the accommodation requirement is met
  • How the relationship requirement is met
  • The development of the applicant and UK partner’s relationship
  • That the UK partner promises to provide the applicant with both the finances and accommodation to satisfy the Immigration Rules
