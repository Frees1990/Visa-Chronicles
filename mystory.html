Hi there again!

I bet you are not looking forward to all the process and documentation you need to go through to get that Visa, been there done that and I am still am. Spouse Visa is only the first step to getting that permanent residency or even Naturalize and let me tell you, it does not get better but getting the spouse visa out of the way is one step closer to never ever paying anymore visa fees and "YES!" no more paperwork no more nerve raking wait for uncertainty.

There is a separate page on this site called the Visa Process that will give you thee general information about the process and the Document Page that provides a simple checklist on what documents to prepare. Browse through my story to learn more about the process through my personal experience. Our circumstances may not be the same but most of the process are the same.

GOOD LUCK TO YOUR JOURNEY

STEP 1

Gather the documents that you already have and make sure that you are already have booked tests that are requiried for you. Make sure to get your TB test as close as your expected application date as it only has a 6 months validiy.

  • Click Here ... for guidance on what you need too compile
  • Language Test
  • TB TEST

STEP 2

  • Go to GOV.UK and select "APPLY AS PARTNER" (Fill out the Online Application form! Questions in there can be a little tricky and confusing but you don't have to worry . Click Here to watch the walkthrough video on how to fill in those questions.)
  • Don't to forget to print out your check list, Appointment Confirmation and IHS payment confirmation
  • Important!!! Make sure you select a appointment date for your biometrics appropriately, always leave a lot of time to finalize and upload all your documents. Uploading your own documents can help the cost low as letting VFS do it can cost to you a lot of money

STEP 3

Upload all your documents
  • Extra Identification card to leave at the security facility for you to access the building
  • All your documents in a clear folder
  • Photocopy of all the documents that you have uploaded (Make sure that this is in a separate clear folder)
  • Hard Copy (print out) Appointment Letter
  • Hard Copy (print out) Appointment Payment Confirmation
  • Hard Copy (print out) IHS Payment Confirmation
  • Hard Copy (print out) Visa Fee Payment Confirmation
  • Original Passport and Copy

There is a limited capacity of how much you can upload per day, reason why you give enough days prior to your appointment date

This is completely optional but it does help if you pay for text update and courier service
